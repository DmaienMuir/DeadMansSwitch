1. Overview

Dead Man's Switch is a single-player roguelike card game played entirely in a browser — a single self-contained HTML file with no dependencies. It is built on the rules of the card game Switch (also known as Crazy Eights), extended with level-based card powers, roguelike relics, a boss system, and meta-progression across multiple runs.

Each round the player must score enough points before running out of hands. Between rounds they upgrade their deck and collect relics that compound in power as the run progresses.

2. Core Rules

2.1 Legal Plays

A card is legal to play if it matches the current middle card's suit or number. The middle card updates after every play. Playing an Ace or a wild L1+ 9 is always legal.

2.2 Special Mechanics

MechanicDescription
Hand Size Capceil(totalCards / 2) where totalCards = deck + hand + discard combined.
Discards5 free discards per round. Each discard draws 1 replacement card. Paid discards cost 1 hand (or 2 with The Usurer boss).
7 DumpPlaying a 7 opens a suit dump — play any number of cards of that suit in sequence. Only the final card fires its power (3s excepted). If the new middle card after any play is a 7, the dump opens immediately for the next hand.
Same-Number StackMultiple cards of the same number played simultaneously. All stackable powers fire independently per card.
Jack ChainJacks do not consume a hand. Each Jack grants bonus follow-up slots for additional cards matching the last non-Jack's suit or number.
AceAlways playable. Declares the active suit for the next card played.
Wild 9 (L1+)Matches any suit or number. Continues dumps. Power fires if it is the last card.
Full Hand Bonus×3 multiplier if every card in hand is played in one turn — applied after all other bonuses.

5. Card Powers

Stackable powers (★) fire independently for each copy of that card in a same-number stack or trailing chain. Queen powers stack additively by default; multiplicatively with Blood Pact.

Card Stack L1 L2 L3
A — Always playable. Declare active suit for the next card. Declare + draw 1 card. Declare + draw 2 cards.
2 ★ Draw 2 cards. Draw 4 cards. Draw 6 cards.
3 ★ +3% free relic chance at round end. +6%. +9%.
4 — Return 1 card from discard pile to hand. Return up to 2 cards. Return up to 3 cards.
5 — Passive: +50 pts per hand while held in hand (not played). +100 pts per hand. +150 pts per hand.
6 ★ Recover 1 free discard. Recover 2 free discards. Grant +2 extra discards beyond the cap.
7 — Opens a suit dump. If 7 becomes new middle, dump opens next hand. Dump scores ×1.5. Dump scores ×2.
8 ★ +1 hand. +2 hands. +3 hands.
9 ★ Wild suit — matches anything. No other effect at L1. Wild + reshuffle hand into deck, draw 7. Wild + reshuffle hand, draw 9.
10 ★ +100 pts added to next hand scored (Surge). +200 pts Surge. +300 pts Surge.
J — Free play (no hand cost) + 1 bonus slot. Free play + draw 1 + 1 bonus slot. Free play + draw 1 + 2 bonus slots.
Q ★ Next hand ×2 multiplier (additive). +×3 per queen played. +×4 per queen played.
K ★ Draw 5 cards. Draw 6 cards. Draw 7 cards.
Jack Bonus Slots: After a Jack, bonus slots let you stage additional cards matching the suit or number of the last non-Jack staged card. L1/L2 = 1 slot; L3 = 2 slots.
Queen Stacking: Default is additive — playing 3× L2 Queens = ×9 on the next hand. With Blood Pact relic this becomes multiplicative (3× L2 = ×27), but each Queen costs 1 extra hand.

One relic is chosen before round 1 from a pool of 3 (no legendaries at start). More are earned through boss rounds and Lucky 3 card power. Relics persist for the entire run.

7. Round Structure

7.1 Tier System

  • 24 base game rounds — Tiers 1–8, 3 rounds per tier.
  • Round 3 of each tier is a Boss Round.
  • 30 endless mode rounds — Tiers 9–18 (rounds 25–54) — unlocked after clearing Tier 8.
  • Round 54 ends the endless run.

7.3 Reward Screen

After each round, choose 2 rewards (3 with The Vault relic):

  • Add Card — add 1 card to your deck
  • Remove Cards — remove up to 2 cards from your deck
  • Upgrade Card — upgrade 1 card by 1 level (max L3)
  • Gain Relic — choose 1 relic (boss rounds only, not round 24)

Boss wins also grant +1 reroll charge, usable on any reward screen option.

Lucky 3: if 3-card powers have accumulated sufficient chance, a free bonus relic pick fires at round end.

The Void: each deck reshuffle during the round grants an additional free relic pick at round end.

9. Boss Assignment

  • Base game: 22 bosses shuffled, 8 assigned to rounds 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24.
  • Endless mode: fresh shuffle, assigned to rounds 27, 30, 33 … 54 (wraps if pool exhausted).
  • Dynamic fields (cursed number, Joker count & penalty, Performance Anxiety relic count, Glass Ceiling cap) resolved when the round starts via applyBossEffects().
  • Debug: forced boss overrides via the debug panel write directly to G.bossSchedule, so isBossRound() returns true and the full boss flow applies.

10. Legacy System (Meta-Progression)

10.1 Relic Memory

For every 3 bosses beaten in a run, the player earns 1 legacy relic pick. On clicking Play Again, the player is shown up to 3 of their current relics and chooses 1 to carry into the next run. 6 bosses cleared = 2 picks; 9 bosses = 3 picks, and so on.

Carried relics are active from the very first hand of the new run, before the player's starting relic pick.

10.2 Persistence

Legacy progress (carried relics and lifetime boss clear count) is stored in localStorage under the key dms_legacy_v1 as JSON. It persists across page visits in the same browser.

Players can export their save data as a JSON string (copied to clipboard) and import it on another browser or device. A reset option clears all legacy progress from the title screen.

Save format: { "legacyRelics": ["blood_pact", "sniper"], "totalBossesCleared": 9 }
