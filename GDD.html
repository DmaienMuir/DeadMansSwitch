DEAD MAN'S SWITCH

Game Design Document  ·  v0.7.10

Overview

Dead Man's Switch is a single-player roguelike deck-builder built on the rules of Switch (also known as Crazy Eights). Play through 24 rounds, each with a score target to beat using a limited number of hands and discards. Between rounds choose rewards — new cards, upgrades, or relics — that permanently shape your run.

Cards have levels (L0–L3) that unlock and enhance powers. Relics provide passive or triggered bonuses. Strategy comes from building synergies between card levels, relic combinations, and the timing and sequencing of your plays.

Core Rules

Basic Play

  • Play one card (or a legal group of cards) per hand. A card is legal if it matches the middle card by suit or by number.
  • After every play, draw back up to your hand size cap.
  • Each round starts with 8 hands and 4 free discards.
  • Discards beyond the free limit cost 1 hand each (2 hands against The Usurer boss).
  • Staged cards cannot be discarded.
  • Double-clicking or double-tapping a legal card stages it directly from hand.

Hand Size Cap

Maximum hand size = ⌈totalCards / 2⌉ where totalCards = deck + hand + discard pile. The Pack Rat relic adds +4 on top of this.

Special Card Mechanics

  • Aces — Always legal to play. Declare the active suit for the next card.
  • 7s — Open a suit dump. See Dump Rules below.
  • Jacks — Always legal after another Jack. Do not cost a hand count.
  • Same-number stacks — Multiple cards of the same number may be played as one hand simultaneously.

7-Dump Rules

  • Strict suit-only — each card must match the opening 7's suit.
  • Jacks may be played mid-dump: if the Jack matches the dump suit it stays in the dump; otherwise it exits the dump and enters jack chain mode.
  • Aces are always chainable in a dump. An off-suit Ace is terminal — only further Aces may follow.
  • A second 7 is only legal immediately after another 7.
  • An off-suit same-number play transitions to samenum mode (locked to that number only).
  • Only the final card fires its power effect — except Threes, which always fire (see Card Powers).
  • If the dump ends on a trailing same-number stack of stackable card types, each card in that stack fires its power independently.

Jack Chain Rules

  • Jacks do not consume a hand.
  • Any Jack is always stageable after another Jack.
  • Non-J/A cards staged after a Jack must match the Jack's suit.
  • Bonus slots (L2/L3 Jacks): An L2 Jack grants 1 bonus staging slot; an L3 Jack grants 2. In a bonus slot the next card may match by suit or number of the last non-Jack staged card. Each bonus slot is consumed when used.
  • An Ace staged after a Jack enters ace mode — only further Aces may follow.

Scoring Order

Each hand is scored in the following sequence. All additive bonuses accumulate into raw before any multiplier is applied.

  1. Base card scores (face value × 10, or 110 for A/J/Q/K)
  2. Philosopher's Stone — raise raw to minimum 100 × card count if below
  3. Per-card number bonuses — Dusty Tome (+50 per J), Tin Star (+50 per Q)
  4. Per-card suit bonuses — Heart of Gold, Club House, Spade Work, Diamond Eye (+20 per matching card)
  5. Odd Fit — +40 per card that matched by number only (not suit)
  6. On Yersel Hen! — +100 per unplayed Queen currently held in hand
  7. The Anvil — +150 if the played card (single-card hand only) matches the middle card's number
  8. Daisy Chain — +25 per card after the first in any multi-card hand
  9. Surge bonus — carried forward from previous 10-power play
  10. 5 passive — +50/100/150 per L1/L2/L3 Five held in hand (not played), added to raw before multipliers
  11. Hand-size multiplier — ×1.5 (3 cards) / ×2 (5) / ×3 (7) / ×5 (10+)
  12. White Rab — largest same-number group within the hand multiplies score (2 matching = ×2, 3 = ×3 …)
  13. The Fool — ×2 (first hand of round only)
  14. Sniper — ×3 (single-card hands only)
  15. Diminishing boss — progressive reduction (Hand 1 = 100 %, Hand 2 = 90 % … floor 30 %)
  16. Queen multiplier — multiplicative stack of all active Queen mults
  17. Full Hand ×3 — if every card in hand was played at once

For 7-dump hands, dump multipliers (from L2/L3 7s) are applied to the entire dump total and are factored in before the hand reaches applyScore.

Reward Flow

After clearing a round you choose 2 rewards (3 with The Vault active) from the following pool:

  • Add — add one card to your deck (every round). Boss rounds always offer minimum L1 cards.
  • Subtract — remove up to 2 cards from your deck (every round).
  • Upgrade — upgrade one card in your deck by one level, max L3 (every round).
  • Relic — choose one of 3 relics to keep permanently (boss rounds only).

The Cunts got cairds? relic extends Add and Upgrade selections to up to 2 cards each. Reward screens default to showing 6 options; Night Market extends this to 8.

Lucky Three

Accumulated free-relic chance from 3-power plays is rolled at round end. On a success, a bonus screen appears before the normal reward screen where you pick one of three relics for free. Reroll charges can be used on this screen.

Reroll Charges

Each boss victory grants +1 reroll charge. Fuck That grants 3 charges on acquisition. Charges can be spent on any reward screen, including Lucky Three, to re-generate the options.

Boss Encounters

One boss is assigned to each of the eight boss rounds (3, 6, 9 … 24) in a shuffled sequence. The boss's target modifier is applied on top of any relic effects.
