Overview

Dead Man's Switch is a single-player roguelike deckbuilder built on the rules of the card game Switch (also known as Crazy Eights). Each run consists of 24 rounds. Every round, the player must score a target number of points by playing cards from their hand onto a middle card, following Switch matching rules.

Between rounds the player improves their deck by adding, removing, and upgrading cards, and collects relics that grant persistent bonuses. Boss rounds occur every third round and impose additional modifiers.

Core Rules

Playing Cards

Each turn costs one hand. A card is legal to play if it matches the middle card by number or suit. Aces are always legal. Playing a card updates the middle card and draws back up to 7 cards (top-up draw).

Multi-Card Plays

Multiple cards may be staged and played as a single hand. Three play modes exist:

ModeTriggerRule
7 DumpFirst card is a 7, or middle is a 7Each subsequent card must match the previous card's suit or number. Only the final card fires its power. The opening 7's level sets the dump score multiplier.
Same NumberMultiple copies of same numberAll cards must share the same number. Each card fires its power independently.
Jack ChainFirst card(s) are JacksLeading Jacks are free (no hand cost). After Jacks, remaining card(s) follow normal staging rules from the Jack's suit.

Discard

Cards may be discarded at any time during a round. The first 4 discards each round are free. Further discards cost 1 hand each. Every discard always draws exactly 1 replacement card, regardless of hand cap or boss rules.

Important: Staged cards cannot be discarded. All other hand cards may be discarded freely while cards are in the play zone.

Aces

Aces are always playable in any context — including mid-dump and after a Jack chain. Playing an Ace ends any active chain (dump or Jack sequence). On play, the player declares a suit, which becomes the active suit for the next card. If the round is already won, suit declaration is skipped.

Jacks

Jacks do not consume a hand count. Multiple Jacks may be chained. After Jacks resolve, the remaining hand follows suit from the final Jack — or an Ace may be played, which ends the chain. At L2, a Jack grants 1 additional free card slot; at L3, 2 additional free card slots. These bonus slots follow the Jack's suit (or dump rules if inside a 7 dump).

Hand Size Cap

The hand cannot exceed half the total deck size (deck + hand + discard pile combined), rounded up. This cap adjusts dynamically as cards are added or removed via rewards. Discard replacement draws are unconditional and bypass this check.

Deck Exhaustion

When the deck is empty, the discard pile is shuffled back in. This can happen at most once per round unless the Eclipse relic or The Void relic is active.

Card Powers

Power cards have effects that activate on play. Effects scale with card level. L0 cards have no power (except Aces, Jacks, and 7s which have intrinsic Switch rules).

CardL1L2L3
A — Ace Declare active suit for next card. Declare suit + draw 1 card. Wild (always legal). Declare suit + draw 2 cards.
2 — Draw Draw 2 Draw 4 Draw 6
3 — Peek View top 2 of deck View & reorder top 4 View top 5, take 1 to hand
4 — Salvage Return 1 card from discard pile to hand. (4s cannot salvage other 4s.) Return 2 cards from discard pile to hand. Return 2 cards from discard pile + draw 1 from deck.
5 — Tempo Next same-suit card costs 0 hands. Next 2 same-suit cards cost 0 hands. Next 2 same-suit cards cost 0 hands + next hand scored gains +100 pts.
6 — Mend Recover 1 used free discard. Recover 2 used free discards. Recover all used free discards.
7 — Dump Open a suit dump (no mult). Last card in chain fires its power. Dump mult ×1.5 Dump mult ×2
8 — Hand+ +1 hand remaining. +2 hands remaining. +2 hands remaining + draw 1 card.
9 — Reset Reshuffle hand into deck, draw 7 fresh cards. Reshuffle hand into deck, draw 9 fresh cards. Reshuffle hand, draw 9, then peek at the top 3 cards of the deck.
10 — Surge +100 pts added to the next hand scored. +200 pts added to the next hand scored. +300 pts added to the next hand scored + grant +1 hand.
J — Jack Free play (no hand cost). Free play + 1 bonus free card slot (same suit or dump rules if in dump). Free play + 2 bonus free card slots.
Q — Queen Next hand scores ×2. Next hand scores ×3. Next hand scores ×4, effect persists for 2 hands.
K — King Draw 5. (Black Kings start at L1; red Kings start at L0.) Draw 6. Draw 7.

7 Dump: In a dump chain, only the final card fires its power. An Ace may be played at any point in a dump — it ends the dump chain immediately. The level of the opening 7 determines the dump score multiplier. Jack bonus slots work within a dump — the dump's suit rules remain active through the Jack's free slots.

05

Card Levels

Every card has a level from L0 to L3. Level affects power strength and visual appearance. Upgrading is a core reward action.

LevelVisualNotes
L0White — no gradientNo power effect. Starting level for 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10, Q. Red Kings.
L1Steel-grey diagonal gradient, silver borderBasic power active. Starting level for A, 2, 7, 8, J. Black Kings.
L2Gold diagonal gradient, gold borderUpgraded power.
L3Blue diagonal gradient, blue borderMaximum power. Cap for all cards.

Always L1 on deal

A, 2, 7, 8, J — these cards always enter the deck at L1.

Start at L0

3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10, Q — start at L0 (no power until upgraded).

King exception

Black Kings (♠, ♣) start at L1. Red Kings (♥, ♦) start at L0.

Hexer boss curse

When the Hexer is active, one randomly chosen card number is treated as L0 for the round, regardless of its actual level.

Double Jump relic

The next upgrade action jumps +2 levels (e.g. L0 → L2). Consumed on use.

06

Progression

The run lasts 24 rounds, with boss rounds at every 3rd. Difficulty increases via score targets and boss modifiers. The deck evolves through reward choices between rounds.

Deck Composition

The player begins with a standard 52-card deck. Cards are added, removed, and upgraded through rewards. Card counts directly affect draw probability, so deck thinning is a meaningful strategy.

07

Reward Flow

After each won round, the player is offered 2 choices drawn from the available action pool. Choices are selected, then confirmed before applying (except Relic, which applies immediately on click).

ActionAvailableDescription
Add Card Every round Pick one card to add to the deck from a generated selection. On boss rounds, only L1+ cards are offered (no L0 cards).
Remove Card Every round Pick one card from the deck to permanently remove. Deck thinning improves draw consistency.
Upgrade Card Every round Pick one card from the deck to upgrade by one level (L0→L1→L2→L3). Cards at L3 cannot be upgraded further.
Relic Boss rounds only Choose one of three offered relics. Applied immediately on selection, no confirmation step.

Starting Relic

Before round 1, the player picks one starting relic from a pool of 3 offered choices. Legendary relics are not available in the starting pool.

Rerolling

Players accumulate reroll charges from two sources: the Fuck That relic grants +3 charges on acquisition, and defeating a boss grants +1 charge. All charges are pooled together. When charges remain, a reroll button appears on reward action screens. Fuck That is removed from inventory when its contributed charges are exhausted, but any bonus charges from boss wins persist independently.

10

Scoring Order

The following steps are applied in sequence when a hand is scored. Additive bonuses are applied before multipliers.

  1. Base card scores — face value × 10 pts per card
  2. Philosopher's Stone floor — minimum 100 pts enforced after base
  3. Per-card number bonuses — Dusty Tome, Tin Star, Old Boots, Worn Compass
  4. Suit bonuses — Heart of Gold, Club House, Spade Work, Diamond Eye, Crimson Tide
  5. Fever — +50 pts if this is the first hand of the round
  6. Top Hat — draw cards on number match (no score bonus)
  7. Odd Fit — +40 pts per number-only match card
  8. On Yersel Hen! — +100 pts per unplayed Queen in hand
  9. Fox's Gambit — draw 1 card per Jack played (no score bonus)
  10. The Anvil — +150 pts on single-card number match
  11. Tally Stick — +100 pts on every 3rd hand played
  12. Daisy Chain — +25 pts per card after the first in multi-card play
  13. Copper Ring — +50 pts for same-number stack of exactly 2
  14. Surge bonus — 6-card power bonus added
  15. Tempo bonus — 10-card power bonus added
  16. Seedling — dump chain per-card bonus
  17. → Hand-size multiplier — ×1.5 / ×2 / ×3 / ×5
  18. → White Rab — ×1.25 on single-card hands
  19. → The Tailor — ×1.5 (once per round)
  20. → The Fool — ×2 (once per round)
  21. → Sniper — ×3 on exact single-card match
  22. → Diminishing boss — applies Eroder reduction
  23. → Queen mult — ×2 if Queen was played previous hand
  24. → Full Hand bonus — ×3 if every card in hand was played
